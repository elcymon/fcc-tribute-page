General William Booth

Some highlights of William Booth's life

William Booth, founder and first general of The Salvation Army, is an inspiration to many people of his day and today. This is not only because of his work in his evangelistic work, he is also remembered for his numerous works in helping people overcome moral and societal failures. His work has helped people overcome alcohol adiction, prostitution, homelessness, poverty, discrimination among other ills of his time.

  • 2 July, 1865: William was born in Sneinton, Nottingham, UK to Samuel and Mary Booth
  • 1842: Left school to become a pawnbroker apprentice
  • 1844: Booth was converted to Methodism. Started reading the bible extensively and engaging in evangelism
  • 1849: Moved to London to work with a pawnbroker and continue with evangelistic, lay preacher, work
  • 10 April, 1852: Leaves pawnbroking job to become a full-time preacher with the Methodist Reform Church
  • 16 July 1855: Married his fiance Catherine Mumford.
  • 1865: Booth and his wife Catherine opens Christian Revival Society in East End of London, which they later renamed to East London Christian Mission
  • May 1878: Renames East London Christian Mission to The Salvation Army
  • 1878 - 1912 Salvation Army expansion beyond England to Europe, Americas, Africa, Aisia and other continents. Booth also travelled extensively during this period to strengthen the army's work. He also engaged in a large number of humanitarian projects and wrote a number of books concerning the army's rules and conduct.
